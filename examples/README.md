# Examples

## Cases

- [**1986 Alfa Romeo Spider (Series 3)**](./1986_alfa_romeo_spider.md) —
  signals span $6.7K – $9.3K with P<sub>5</sub> = $8,700; both dialogues
  end inside 3% of the $8,000 sale price after substantive challenges on
  originality verification and trim ambiguity.
- [**1986 Chevrolet Corvette (C4)**](./1986_chevrolet_corvette_c4.md) —
  P<sub>1</sub> sits at $13.4K (ask-driven) while P<sub>3</sub>/P<sub>4</sub>
  pull P<sub>5</sub> down to $6,700 — the realized sale price exactly. The
  integrative dialogue holds at $6,700; the distributive dialogue removes an
  unverified originality premium and lands at $6,200.
- [**2009 BMW 135i (E8x)**](./2009_bmw_135i.md) — fast clean
  convergence: P<sub>5</sub> = $19,500 above the four signals' median, both
  dialogues settle in three rounds within 3% of the $18,550 sale price.
- [**2019 Ford Shelby GT350R (S550)**](./2019_ford_shelby_gt350r.md) —
  high-end enthusiast car (low-mileage GT350R), signals tight at $76K–$87K;
  D and I both land within 5% of the $80,000 sale price.
- [**1996 Land Rover Defender 90 (L316)**](./1996_land_rover_defender_90.md) —
  the full ten-round distributive dialogue: the operator successively
  challenges the engine identification, an "originality verified"
  overstatement, the convertible configuration, mileage documentation, and
  steering-side uncertainty; the Guide explicitly owns several reasoning
  errors ("$1,500 was a placation move") and lands at $63,500 — the
  realized sale price exactly. The integrative dialogue converges in four
  rounds at $57,000.

Each transcript is verbatim from the run logs; each Guide turn shows its
revised P<sub>5</sub> with a delta arrow against the prior round.
